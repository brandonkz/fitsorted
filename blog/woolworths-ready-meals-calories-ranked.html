Why This Matters

Woolworths ready meals are everywhere. They're convenient, they taste decent, and when you're too tired to cook, they're the default. But if you're trying to lose weight, gain muscle, or just eat within a calorie budget, you need to know what you're actually getting.

A Thai Green Curry has 650 calories. A Chicken & Mushroom Pasta has 820. That's a 170-calorie difference for what looks like the same kind of meal. If you're guessing, you're losing.

The Woolworths Ready Meal Strategy

Here's how to actually use this info:

If You're Losing Weight

  • Stick to meals under 400 calories
  • Pair with a side salad or extra veg to increase volume without calories
  • Avoid anything with "mash," "pasta," or "creamy" in the name
  • Track every meal - don't assume "healthy-looking" means low-calorie

If You're Gaining Muscle

  • Look for 500-650 calorie meals with high protein content
  • Add a protein shake or Greek yogurt on the side to hit your macros
  • Beef, lamb, and chicken meals are your best bets
  • Avoid salads unless you're adding your own protein

If You're on a Budget

  • Mac & Cheese with Bacon is the cheapest per calorie (R7.64/100kcal)
  • Spaghetti Bolognese is solid value at R9.32/100kcal
  • Skip the salmon and lamb - you're paying double for the same satiety
  • Buy in bulk when on special and freeze

The Problem with Guessing

Most people grab whatever looks good, heat it up, and assume they're eating around 400-500 calories. Then they wonder why they're not losing weight.

Here's the reality: a Lamb Shank with Mash is 850 calories. A Grilled Chicken Salad is 310. That's a 540-calorie difference. If you're eating Woolworths meals 5 days a week and guessing wrong each time, you're off by 2,700 calories per week. That's nearly a full day's worth of food you didn't account for.

This is why tracking matters. Not obsessively. Not forever. But at least until you know what you're actually putting in your body.
