How Much Protein Do South Africans Actually Need? (Calculator + Guide)

Most people either under-eat protein and lose muscle, or overpay for it because they don't know the actual numbers. Here's exactly how much you need based on your bodyweight, and the cheapest SA protein sources to hit your target.

The Quick Answer

If you're training (weights, running, sports, anything that breaks down muscle), you need 1.6-2.2 grams of protein per kilogram of bodyweight per day.

That's the range backed by actual research. Not bro-science. Not Instagram fitness influencers. Actual peer-reviewed studies on muscle protein synthesis and recovery.

If you weigh 70kg and you train regularly, you need 112-154g of protein per day. If you're eating less than that, you're making it harder to build or maintain muscle. If you're eating way more, you're just making expensive pee.

Why Protein Actually Matters

Protein is made of amino acids. Amino acids are the building blocks your body uses to repair muscle tissue after you train. If you don't eat enough protein, your body can't rebuild what you broke down in the gym.

That means:

  • Slower muscle recovery
  • Less strength gains over time
  • Higher risk of losing muscle when cutting calories
  • Feeling hungrier because protein keeps you full longer than carbs or fat

This isn't just for bodybuilders. If you run, play rugby, do CrossFit, or even just lift 3 times a week, you're breaking down muscle tissue. You need protein to recover properly.

Common Protein Mistakes

❌ Mistake 1: Only eating protein at dinner

Your body can only absorb about 30-40g of protein at once. If you eat 150g of protein in one meal, you're wasting most of it. Spread it out: 30-40g at breakfast, lunch, and dinner.

❌ Mistake 2: Thinking you need 2g per pound

That's 2g per kilogram, not pound. If you weigh 70kg, you don't need 140g (2×70lb = 154lb), you need 112-154g. Big difference.

❌ Mistake 3: Buying expensive protein when cheap works fine

Biltong is R44.99 for 30g of protein. Eggs are R9.61 for the same amount. Your muscles don't care which one you ate. Save your money.

❌ Mistake 4: Not tracking protein intake

Most people think they're eating enough protein. Then they actually track it and realize they're getting 60-80g per day instead of 150g. Track for at least a week to see where you really stand.

How to Hit Your Protein Target Every Day

Here's a simple 3-meal framework to hit 150g protein without thinking too hard:

Breakfast (40g protein)

  • 4 eggs scrambled (52g protein) OR
  • Greek yogurt (200g) + 1 scoop protein powder (44g protein) OR
  • 3 eggs + 2 slices toast with peanut butter (42g protein)

Lunch (50g protein)

  • 200g grilled chicken + veggies (62g protein) OR
  • 2 tuna cans + salad (50g protein) OR
  • Woolworths Grilled Chicken Salad (38g) + boiled eggs (26g) = 64g protein

Dinner (50g protein)

  • 200g chicken breast + rice + veg (62g protein) OR
  • Woolworths Beef Stroganoff (40g) + cottage cheese side (12g) = 52g protein OR
  • Lamb chops (150g) + sweet potato (48g protein)

Total: 140-160g protein across 3 meals. No supplements needed if you plan ahead.
