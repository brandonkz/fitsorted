FitSorted Launches South Africa's First WhatsApp-Based Calorie Tracker

Cape Town, South Africa — FitSorted, a new health-tech product from AlphaX Asset Capital, today announced the launch of South Africa's first AI-powered calorie and macro tracker that operates entirely through WhatsApp. No app download required.

The Problem: Tracking Calories Shouldn't Be This Hard

South Africa has one of the highest obesity rates in sub-Saharan Africa, yet the most popular calorie-tracking apps remain expensive, complex, and built for American and European markets. MyFitnessPal charges upwards of R350/month for premium features. Most apps don't know that a Nando's quarter chicken is 429 calories or that a Kauai Peanut Butter Bomb packs 764.

Meanwhile, WhatsApp reaches over 90% of South African smartphone users. FitSorted was built on a simple question: what if tracking your food was as easy as sending a message to a friend?

How FitSorted Works

Users message the FitSorted WhatsApp number (+27 69 068 4940) to get started. After a quick setup — gender, weight, height, age, activity level, and goal — the bot calculates a personalised daily calorie target.

From there, tracking is effortless:

  • Send any food — "2 eggs and toast", "Steers Wacky Wednesday burger", "Woolies chicken wrap" — and get instant calorie and macro breakdowns
  • Morning check-in (6:30 AM) — a personalised greeting with your goal and yesterday's recap
  • Evening summary (8:00 PM) — total calories, protein, carbs, and fat for the day, plus whether you're in a deficit or surplus
  • Coaching mode — ask nutrition questions and get personalised advice based on your profile
  • Exercise logging — track workouts and calories burned
  • Weight tracking — log weigh-ins and see trends over time

Built for South Africa

FitSorted's food database includes hundreds of items from local chains and brands that international apps simply don't cover:

  • Nando's — quarter chicken, peri-peri livers, spicy rice
  • Kauai — smoothies, bowls, wraps with accurate portion data
  • Steers — burgers, ribs, Wacky Wednesday specials
  • Woolworths — ready meals, rotisserie chicken, snacks
  • Everyday SA food — pap and wors, bunny chow, biltong, rusks, koeksisters

Pricing That Makes Sense

FitSorted is currently in free beta. When the paid tier launches, it will cost R36/month — roughly 4x cheaper than competing apps. No long-term contracts. Cancel anytime by sending a WhatsApp message.

"We wanted a price that any South African could justify," said the FitSorted team. "Skip one takeaway coffee a month and your calorie tracking is covered."

Early Traction

Since launching its beta on 3 March 2026, FitSorted has signed up 17 users who have collectively logged over 150 meals. Active users are logging daily, with the most engaged beta tester recording 68 food entries in the first week alone.

The team is focused on refining the experience through user feedback before scaling marketing efforts.

What's Next

FitSorted plans to roll out several features in the coming weeks:

  • Payment integration via PayFast for seamless in-WhatsApp subscription management
  • Influencer referral programme — fitness coaches and creators earn 20% recurring commission on every subscriber they refer
  • Expanded SA food database — ongoing additions based on user requests
  • Weekly progress reports — weight trends, average intake, and consistency scores
