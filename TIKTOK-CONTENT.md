## Content Production Tips

### Equipment Needed
- **Phone camera** (no fancy gear needed)
- **Natural lighting** (film near window or outdoors)
- **Tripod or phone stand** (R150 on Takealot)

### Editing Apps
- **CapCut** (free, easy text overlays)
- **TikTok native editor** (built-in, works fine)

### Filming Tips
- Film vertical (9:16 aspect ratio)
- Keep first 2 seconds punchy (hook or they scroll)
- Add text overlays (40% watch with sound off)
- Use trending sounds when they fit (check TikTok trends weekly)

### Posting Schedule
- **Best times:** 6-9 AM, 12-2 PM, 6-10 PM SAST
- **Frequency:** 5-7 posts per week (consistency > perfection)
- **Day off:** Sunday (or Monday)

## Growth Tactics

### 1. Hook Formula
First 2 seconds must make them stop scrolling:

**Pattern 1:** Question  
"Guess the calories in [SA food]"

**Pattern 2:** Surprise  
"This SA food has MORE calories than a Big Mac"

**Pattern 3:** Relatable  
"POV: You're trying to lose weight in South Africa and..."

**Pattern 4:** Controversy  
"You don't actually need [common fitness advice]"

### 2. Comment Engagement
- Reply to every comment in first hour (boosts reach)
- Ask questions in captions ("Have you tried this?")
- Reply with value, not just "thanks"

### 3. Duet/Stitch Opportunities
- Find fitness influencers saying BS → stitch with facts
- Duet SA food content → add calorie info
- React to "what I eat in a day" videos → calculate their calories

### 4. Series Content
Create series people come back for:

- **"Guess the Calories"** (SA foods)
- **"Fitness Myths Debunked"** (weekly)
- **"R36 vs R200/mo apps"** (FitSorted vs competitors)
