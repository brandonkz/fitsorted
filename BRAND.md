# FitSorted Brand Guidelines

## Mission
Making calorie tracking simple, affordable, and accessible for South Africans—no app required, just WhatsApp.

## Brand Positioning
**We are:** The no-BS calorie tracker that fits your life, not the other way around  
**We are not:** Another expensive fitness app that requires a PhD to use

## Target Audience

**Primary:** South African adults (25-45) who:
- Want to lose weight or track nutrition
- Tired of expensive apps with complicated features
- Already use WhatsApp daily
- Value simplicity over perfection
- Need SA-specific food data (Kauai, Nando's, Steers)

**Psychographics:**
- Practical, not perfectionist
- Budget-conscious (R36/mo vs R100-200/mo competitors)
- Mobile-first (WhatsApp > app downloads)
- Want results without the fluff

## Brand Voice

### Tone: Friendly SA Mate
- Conversational, not clinical
- Encouraging, not preachy
- Direct, not corporate
- South African slang is OK ("howzit", "lekker", "boet")

**Examples:**

✅ **Good:** "Howzit! Log your breakfast and let's see where you're at."  
❌ **Bad:** "Please input your morning meal consumption for nutritional analysis."

✅ **Good:** "You're 300 cal under goal—braai time! 🔥"  
❌ **Bad:** "Caloric deficit detected. Consider additional protein intake."

✅ **Good:** "Two slices of toast and peanut butter = 380 cal. Not bad!"  
❌ **Bad:** "Consumed: 380 calories. Macronutrient breakdown: 12g protein, 48g carbs, 16g fat."

### Personality Traits
- **Supportive** > Judgmental
- **Practical** > Perfectionist
- **Relatable** > Aspirational
- **Honest** > Fluffy

## Visual Identity

### Colors
- **Primary:** Green (#10b981) - Growth, health, progress
- **Secondary:** Dark gray (#1a1a1a) - Solid, trustworthy
- **Accent:** White (#ffffff) - Clean, simple
- **Warning:** Red (#ef4444) - Surplus, over goal
- **Success:** Green (#22c55e) - Deficit, on track

### Typography
- **Primary font:** Inter (clean, modern, readable)
- **Style:** Simple, sans-serif, nothing fancy

### Imagery Style
**Food photos:**
- Real, not stock
- SA-specific (pap, braai, bunny chow, Nando's)
- Phone quality OK (authentic > polished)
- Show actual portions, not Instagram perfect

**People:**
- Real users, not models
- Diverse (reflect SA demographics)
- Everyday settings (not gym glamour shots)
- Before/after OK but no body shaming

## Messaging Pillars

### 1. Simplicity
"No app to download. Just WhatsApp."

**Why it matters:** SA users are app-fatigued. WhatsApp has 90%+ penetration. No friction = more users.

**Content angles:**
- Compare: "Other apps: Download, create account, input data, pay. FitSorted: Just WhatsApp."
- Show: Screen recording of logging food in 10 seconds
- Proof: "5 steps to track calories: 1. WhatsApp us. That's it."

### 2. Affordability
"R36/month. 4x cheaper than competitors."

**Why it matters:** SA economy is tough. Most fitness apps are $10-20/mo (R180-360). We're accessible.

**Content angles:**
- Price comparison graphics (R36 vs competitors)
- "What R36 gets you" (vs what R200/mo gets elsewhere)
- "Skip one coffee = track calories for a month"

### 3. Local Understanding
"Knows your food. Nando's, Kauai, Steers—all in the database."

**Why it matters:** International apps don't have SA chain data. We do.

**Content angles:**
- "Try asking MyFitnessPal for Kauai Peanut Butter Bomb calories" (they won't know)
- Show our SA food database (Nando's quarter chicken, Woolies meals, braai meat)
- "Built for South Africans, by South Africans"

### 4. Results Without the BS
"No diet plans. No meal prep guides. Just track, see your deficit, done."

**Why it matters:** People are tired of apps that overcomplicate. Just help them eat less.

**Content angles:**
- "You don't need a meal plan. You need to know if you're in a deficit."
- Show: "Lost 0.5kg this week by staying 500 cal under goal. That's it."
- Anti-fluff: "We don't sell protein powder. We don't have a 'community'. We just count calories."

## Content Themes

### Theme 1: SA Food Reality
**Content:** Real SA foods, real portions, real calories  
**Examples:**
- "How many Nando's wings can you eat on 1800 cal/day?"
- "Pap and wors: 600 calories. Worth it? Yes."
- "Kauai smoothies: healthy or calorie bomb? Let's check."

### Theme 2: Myth Busting
**Content:** Debunk fitness BS with data  
**Examples:**
- "You don't need to eat 6 meals a day"
- "Carbs don't make you fat. Eating too much does."
- "Cheat days won't ruin your progress (here's the math)"

### Theme 3: Quick Wins
**Content:** Practical tips that actually work  
**Examples:**
- "Swap regular Coke for Coke Zero = 500 cal saved per day"
- "Use a smaller plate = eat 20% less without trying"
- "Log your food BEFORE you eat it (game changer)"

### Theme 4: Relatable Struggles
**Content:** Normalize the hard parts  
**Examples:**
- "POV: You logged everything perfectly until 8pm, then the Woolies chocolate called"
- "When you're 200 cal under goal but your tjommie suggests Steers"
- "Explaining calorie tracking to your ouma who thinks you're starving yourself"

## Call-to-Actions

**Primary CTA:** "WhatsApp +27690684940 to start"

**Secondary CTAs:**
- "Free trial, no credit card"
- "R36/month after 7 days"
- "Cancel anytime from WhatsApp"

**Avoid:**
- "Sign up now!" (no signup, it's WhatsApp)
- "Download the app!" (there is no app)
- "Join our community!" (too corporate, not our vibe)

## Content Don'ts

❌ **Body shaming:** Never mock people for their weight  
❌ **Perfectionism:** Don't show unrealistic results  
❌ **Medical claims:** Never say "lose 5kg in 2 weeks guaranteed"  
❌ **Fitness influencer energy:** No flexing abs, no gym bro talk  
❌ **Diet culture BS:** No "good foods" vs "bad foods" moralizing  
❌ **Guilt tripping:** Never shame users for going over goal  

## Competitive Differentiation

**vs MyFitnessPal:**
- We: WhatsApp (no app)
- They: Complicated UI, expensive premium ($20/mo)

**vs CaloriChat ($100/yr):**
- We: R36/mo (4x cheaper), SA food database, macro tracking
- They: Generic, no local food, 62 users globally

**vs Noom ($60/mo):**
- We: Just calorie tracking
- They: Psychology lessons, expensive, overwhelming

**Our edge:** Simplicity + affordability + local = unbeatable for SA market
